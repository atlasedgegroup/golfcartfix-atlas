Club Car Won't Move: Step-by-Step Diagnosis

Covers DS (36V and 48V) and Precedent. The OBC on 48V models adds a diagnostic step that most guides skip — this one doesn't.

Fast Answer: Check Battery Voltage First

Measure total pack voltage at the main battery terminals. A 48V pack should read 50–52V charged; 36V pack reads 37–38V. If pack voltage is below 34V (48V) or 25V (36V), the OBC or charger threshold is preventing operation. Charge first. If pack voltage is in range, the diagnostic sequence is: solenoid click test → F/R switch → controller → motor.

48V DS (1995–2014)

Check OBC connector first. A loose or corroded OBC 6-pin connector causes no-move and no-charge simultaneously.

Solenoid Click Test

Press pedal, listen for click under seat. No click = upstream fault (switch, key, OBC signal). Click + no move = solenoid or controller.

PDS System (2004+)

PDS Precedent: check the run/tow switch under the seat. Must be in RUN. Also check seat switch — PDS disables drive if seat switch open.

Full Diagnostic Sequence

1. Run/Tow Switch

Every Club Car has a Run/Tow switch under the seat. It must be in the RUN position for the cart to operate. It sounds obvious, but this gets missed constantly — especially after battery maintenance when the switch gets bumped to TOW accidentally. Check it before doing anything else.

2. Battery Pack Voltage

Measure total pack voltage across the full string. For 48V packs (6×8V): should read 50–52V resting after a full charge. For 36V packs (6×6V): 37–38V. Below these ranges, the OBC (on 48V models) will prevent the charger from engaging and may also affect drive circuit activation. Charge the pack first.

3. OBC Check (48V DS 1994.5–2014, Precedent pre-2014)

The OBC is a small black box typically mounted under the seat on the driver's side. It has a 6-pin connector. Disconnect and reconnect this connector — corrosion on the OBC pins is a frequent cause of no-move and no-charge on 48V Club Cars. Clean the connector pins with electrical contact cleaner if there's any green or white corrosion visible.

4. Solenoid Click Test

Press the accelerator with key on and F/R switch in Forward. Listen for a click from the solenoid (under the seat, near the batteries). A click means the solenoid coil is energizing. Test the main contacts with a multimeter in continuity mode across the two large bolt terminals during the click. No continuity = failed solenoid contacts. Replace solenoid ($25–$60).

5. Forward/Reverse Switch

If there's no click: test the F/R switch. On DS models it's under the seat; on Precedent it's in the dash. Disconnect the switch and test continuity in each direction. Failed F/R switch = no solenoid signal regardless of battery state.

6. Controller (IQ / PDS)

If solenoid clicks and closes but cart doesn't move, test voltage at the motor terminals during pedal press. No voltage output from controller = failed controller. Note: on PDS Precedent models, also check the accelerator Hall sensor (the Pedal Position Sensor) — failure here makes the controller see zero throttle request.
