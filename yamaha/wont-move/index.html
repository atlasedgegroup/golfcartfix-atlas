Yamaha Golf Cart Won't Move: Gas and Electric Diagnosis

Covers G-Series, Drive, and Drive2. Gas models: fuel and clutch path. Electric models: battery, solenoid, Run/Tow switch, and controller.

Fast Answer: Gas or Electric?

Electric model: Check the Run/Tow switch first (under the seat — must be in RUN). Then measure pack voltage. A 36V G-series should read 37–38V; 48V Drive/Drive2 should read 50–52V. Low voltage = charge first. Voltage OK = solenoid click test.

Gas model: Does the engine turn over? If engine cranks but won't engage drive — check the centrifugal clutch. If engine won't start at all, that's a fuel/ignition problem before the drivetrain.

Run/Tow Switch

Under the seat. Must be in RUN. Gets bumped to TOW during maintenance — check this first, every time.

Electric: Solenoid

Press pedal. Click? Probe large posts in continuity. No continuity = replace solenoid ($30–$70).

Electric: Seat Switch

Drive2 and some Drive models require weight on the seat to enable drive. Test the seat switch if Run/Tow is in RUN but no solenoid signal.

Gas: Clutch

Engine running at idle, no movement = centrifugal clutch slipping or stuck. Engine at higher RPM = clutch should engage. If not, primary clutch weights may be stuck.

Electric Model Diagnosis

1. Run/Tow and Seat Switch

The Run/Tow switch under the seat is the most-missed item in any Yamaha diagnosis. In TOW mode, the controller is disabled to allow free-wheeling during transport. Also check the seat switch on Drive and Drive2 models — some configurations disable drive when no weight is detected on the seat (a safety feature). Test the seat switch with a multimeter in continuity mode: should close when pressed.

2. Battery Pack Voltage

Yamaha G-series electric uses 36V (6×6V batteries). Drive and Drive2 use 48V (6×8V). Measure total pack voltage. G-series: should read 37–38V charged. Drive/Drive2: 50–52V charged. If low, charge fully and retest before proceeding.

3. Solenoid Click Test

Press the accelerator with key on. Listen for a click from the solenoid under the seat. On G-series electric, the solenoid is clearly visible near the battery bank. On Drive/Drive2, it's more tucked in. A click means the control circuit is working. Test the main contacts in continuity mode during the click. No continuity = solenoid contact failure. Replace for $30–$70.

4. Controller (Drive/Drive2 Specific)

On the Yamaha Drive (DC motor), if the solenoid closes but no movement: probe the motor terminals for output voltage. No output = controller failure. On the Drive2 (AC motor): the AC controller and motor are more integrated. If the cart shows an error indicator light on the dash, note the blink pattern before doing any work — Yamaha Drive2 error codes are specific enough to isolate the fault to battery, motor, or controller.

Gas Model Diagnosis (G-Series, Drive Gas)

Engine Starts, Cart Doesn't Move

If the engine starts and runs but the cart doesn't move, the issue is in the drivetrain: centrifugal clutch (primary), driven clutch (secondary), or the belt connecting them. At idle, the centrifugal clutch should be disengaged. As you press the pedal and engine RPM rises, the clutch weights fly out and engage the belt. If you rev the engine and still no movement, the clutch weights are likely seized or stuck.
