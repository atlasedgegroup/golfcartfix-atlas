E-Z-GO Controller Symptoms and Diagnosis

Seven controller failure symptoms, how to test output with a multimeter, and replacement options for TXT (Curtis/Alltrax) and RXV controllers.

Fast Answer: Confirm Before You Replace

Controllers are the most expensive component in the drivetrain ($200–$700). Do not replace one until you've confirmed: (1) battery voltage is correct, (2) solenoid is closing, and (3) the controller is receiving input signal from the ITS/throttle. If all three are true and the motor gets no power, the controller is likely failed.

No Output Voltage

Probe M terminal during pedal press. Zero volts with solenoid closed = controller failure.

Jerky Movement

Throttle sensor (ITS) issue or speed sensor feedback fault. Test ITS resistance before replacing controller.

Overheating

Controller gets hot to touch within 5 min. Could be internal fault or excessive current from locked motor.

RXV Error Codes

Note the LED blink pattern on the dash before any work. Codes isolate the fault without guessing.

Controller Failure Symptoms

1. No Movement Despite Solenoid Clicking

The solenoid clicks (confirmed by continuity test on large posts) but the cart doesn't move. Set multimeter to DC voltage. Probe the B+ terminal and the M (motor) terminal on the controller while a helper presses the pedal. A working controller will ramp voltage from 0 to near pack voltage as the pedal is depressed. Zero volts at M with pack voltage at B+ and solenoid closed = failed controller.

2. Jerky or Uneven Speed

Cart moves but lurches, surges, or has a dead zone in the throttle. Usually this is the Inductive Throttle Sensor (ITS) rather than the controller itself. The ITS is the sensor mounted on the pedal assembly. Test it: set multimeter to resistance (ohms) and probe the ITS terminals while slowly pressing the pedal. Resistance should rise smoothly from ~0 to 5k ohms. If it jumps or has a gap, replace the ITS ($25–$60) before buying a controller.

3. Cart Cuts Out at Speed

Cart runs normally then cuts out under load (climbing a hill or starting from rest). This can be a controller thermal shutdown (overheating), a battery voltage sag below the low-voltage cutoff, or a loose B+ connection to the controller. Check all controller terminal bolts first — a loose B+ terminal causes intermittent cutout and can arc, burning the terminal lug.

4. Full Speed Only, No Throttle Control

Cart launches at full speed when the pedal is barely touched. This indicates the ITS or the controller's internal throttle circuit has failed open-high. Test the ITS first. If ITS is good, the controller's throttle input stage is failed.

5. Burning Smell from Controller Area

A burning electronics smell (distinct from battery off-gassing, which smells like sulfur) means the controller is internally arcing or a MOSFET has failed. At this point the controller is almost certainly beyond repair. Continued operation risks motor damage.

Replacement Options

For the EZGO TXT, Curtis and Alltrax are the two most common controller brands. Alltrax is popular because it offers programmable parameters (max speed, throttle curve) and is highly reliable. Curtis controllers are OEM and widely available used. For the RXV, the controller is proprietary — you're looking at OEM replacement or a rebuild service.
