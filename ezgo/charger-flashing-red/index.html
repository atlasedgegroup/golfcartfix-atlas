E-Z-GO Charger Flashing Red: What the Codes Mean

Covers DeltaQ QuiQ (2010+ TXT), PowerWise (pre-2010 TXT), and RXV chargers. Flash codes translated, with battery recovery procedures for packs too dead to trigger a charge.

Fast Answer

The most common cause of a red-flashing charger is a battery pack that's dropped below the charger's activation threshold. The DeltaQ won't begin a charge cycle if pack voltage is below ~34V (48V pack) or ~25V (36V pack). If the pack has sat discharged for weeks, you may need to manually trickle-charge individual batteries to bring them up before the charger will engage.

Flash Code 1 (DeltaQ)

Battery voltage too low to start charge. Manually boost individual cells to ~6V each, then retry charger.

Flash Code 2

Charge timeout — pack couldn't reach full voltage within time limit. Usually a weak or failing battery.

Flash Code 3

High battery temperature. Let pack cool 2 hours before retrying. Check for shorted cell.

Solid Red (PowerWise)

Fault condition. Try disconnecting AC and DC for 5 min. If it persists, test pack voltage — below threshold is the usual culprit.

DeltaQ QuiQ Flash Codes (2010+ EZGO TXT)

The DeltaQ QuiQ charger uses a red LED that flashes in sequences to indicate fault conditions. Count the number of flashes in each burst, pause, then count again to confirm the code.

Manual Battery Recovery Procedure (Flash Code 1)

When pack voltage is too low for the DeltaQ to engage, you need to manually bring each battery up to a minimum voltage. This requires a 12V automotive battery charger or a small trickle charger.

  1. Identify the battery configuration. Open the seat. You'll see either six 6V batteries (36V pack) or six 8V batteries (48V pack) connected in series.
  2. Test each battery individually. Measure voltage across each battery's own terminals. Any battery below 4V needs individual charging. Below 2V is likely sulfated and may not recover.
  3. Connect a 12V trickle charger across one low battery. Set to 2A max. Charge for 30–60 minutes until it reads at least 5.5V (6V battery) or 7V (8V battery).
  4. Repeat for each low battery. You don't need them fully charged — just above the DeltaQ's minimum threshold.
  5. Reconnect the DeltaQ charger. Total pack should now be above the activation threshold and the charger should begin its normal charge cycle.
⚠ Safety note: Never charge batteries in an enclosed space. Lead-acid batteries off-gas hydrogen during charging. Keep open flames and sparks away from the battery compartment.

PowerWise Charger (Pre-2010 EZGO TXT)

The older PowerWise charger doesn't have flash codes — it shows a solid LED status. A solid red or no-charge condition usually means the pack voltage is below the 26V minimum (36V system). The same manual recovery procedure above applies. PowerWise chargers have a simpler design and are more tolerant of marginal pack voltage, but they also fail more often with age. A 15+ year old PowerWise that's not charging is as likely to be charger failure as battery failure.
