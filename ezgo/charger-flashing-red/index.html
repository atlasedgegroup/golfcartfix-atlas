E-Z-GO Charger Flashing Red: What the Codes Mean

Covers DeltaQ QuiQ (2010+ TXT), PowerWise (pre-2010 TXT), and RXV chargers. Flash codes translated, with battery recovery procedures for packs too dead to trigger a charge.

Fast Answer

On a DeltaQ QuiQ charger, the red Fault LED flashes a count from 1 to 6 — count the flashes in each burst to read the code. The two most common causes are a battery-voltage fault (codes 1 and 2) and a pack so deeply discharged the charger can't trickle it up to the level needed to start a normal cycle (code 4, “Check Battery”). For a pack that's sat discharged for weeks, you may need to manually charge the individual batteries first — see the recovery procedure below.

1 flash (DeltaQ)

High battery voltage detected. Confirm the charger's voltage matches the pack, check for wiring errors, and disconnect any other source charging the pack. Clears automatically when corrected.

2 flashes

Low battery voltage detected. Check the battery and all connections, and confirm the pack's nominal voltage matches the charger. Clears automatically when corrected.

3 flashes

Charge timeout — pack didn't reach full charge in the allowed time. Usually a weak, old, or damaged battery. Must be cleared by unplugging AC for 30 seconds.

4 flashes

Check Battery — pack couldn't be trickle-charged up to the minimum needed to start. Look for reversed/loose links or a shorted cell; recover individual batteries (procedure below).

DeltaQ QuiQ Flash Codes (2010+ EZGO TXT)

The DeltaQ QuiQ charger uses a red Fault LED that flashes in sequences to indicate fault conditions. Count the number of flashes in each burst, pause, then count again to confirm the code. The flash counts below follow Delta-Q's official QuiQ troubleshooting guide. (Note: low AC voltage and high internal temperature are shown on the separate AC and Ammeter LEDs, not as a red Fault flash code.)

Manual Battery Recovery Procedure (Check Battery / won't-start)

When the pack has fallen too low for the DeltaQ to trickle it up to a normal charge (the “Check Battery” / 4-flash condition, or the charger simply won't start a cycle), you need to manually bring each battery up first. This requires a 12V automotive battery charger or a small trickle charger.

  1. Identify the battery configuration. Open the seat. You'll see either six 6V batteries (36V pack) or six 8V batteries (48V pack) connected in series.
  2. Test each battery individually. Measure voltage across each battery's own terminals. Any battery below 4V needs individual charging. Below 2V is likely sulfated and may not recover.
  3. Connect a 12V trickle charger across one low battery. Set to 2A max. Charge for 30–60 minutes until it reads at least 5.5V (6V battery) or 7V (8V battery).
  4. Repeat for each low battery. You don't need them fully charged — just above the DeltaQ's minimum threshold.
  5. Reconnect the DeltaQ charger. Total pack should now be above the activation threshold and the charger should begin its normal charge cycle.
⚠ Safety note: Never charge batteries in an enclosed space. Lead-acid batteries off-gas hydrogen during charging. Keep open flames and sparks away from the battery compartment.

PowerWise Charger (Pre-2010 EZGO TXT)

The older PowerWise charger doesn't have flash codes — it shows a solid LED status. A solid red or no-charge condition usually means the pack voltage is below the 26V minimum (36V system). The same manual recovery procedure above applies. PowerWise chargers have a simpler design and are more tolerant of marginal pack voltage, but they also fail more often with age. A 15+ year old PowerWise that's not charging is as likely to be charger failure as battery failure.
