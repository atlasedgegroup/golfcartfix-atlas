E-Z-GO Solenoid Problems: Test and Replace

The solenoid is the first thing to check when an EZGO clicks but doesn't move. Five-minute multimeter test, part numbers, and replacement procedure.

Fast Answer: The Click Test

Press the accelerator pedal. If you hear a single click from under the seat, the solenoid coil is energizing. Now set your multimeter to continuity and probe the two large posts (the thick cable terminals) during that click. No continuity = failed solenoid contact plate. Replace the solenoid. Cost: $20–$60, 30 minutes.

Click + No Move

Solenoid energizes but contact plate fails to close. Replace solenoid.

No Click

Solenoid not getting signal. Check F/R switch, key switch, and pedal microswitch first.

Burning Smell

Contact points arcing internally. Replace immediately — a failed-open solenoid can burn motor windings.

How the EZGO Solenoid Works

The solenoid is an electromagnetic relay with two circuits: a low-current control circuit (typically 12–36V) and a high-current main circuit that connects the battery pack to the controller. When you press the accelerator, the control circuit energizes the solenoid coil, which pulls a copper contact plate down onto two large copper posts, completing the main power circuit.

Solenoids fail in two ways: the contact plate wears or burns (click but no movement) or the coil fails (no click at all). Contact plate failure is far more common.

Locating the Solenoid

On EZGO TXT: lift the seat. The solenoid is usually a black cylindrical unit mounted on the driver's side of the battery tray, near the front. It has four terminals: two small spade connectors (control circuit) and two large bolt terminals (main power).

On EZGO RXV: the solenoid is integrated differently — the RXV uses a solid-state controller that handles switching internally. If you're on an RXV and getting no movement, focus on the controller and its CAN bus signals rather than a traditional solenoid.

Multimeter Test Procedure

  1. Safety first. Turn key off. Put F/R switch in neutral if it has one. Do not work on batteries with metal jewelry on hands or wrists.
  2. Locate the solenoid. Find the black cylindrical relay under the seat. Identify the two small spade terminals (control) and two large bolt terminals (main power).
  3. Test coil resistance. Set multimeter to Ohms. Probe the two small control terminals. A healthy coil reads 10–20 ohms. Open circuit (OL) = failed coil, replace solenoid.
  4. Energize and test main circuit. Turn key on, F/R switch to forward. Have a helper press the pedal while you probe the two large posts with multimeter on continuity. Click + no continuity = failed contact plate, replace solenoid.
  5. Check control voltage. If no click: set multimeter to DC voltage. Probe the small spade terminals during pedal press. Should read pack voltage (36V or 48V). No voltage here = F/R switch or key switch problem upstream.

Replacement

Disconnect both main battery cables from the pack before replacing the solenoid. Match the voltage rating: EZGO TXT uses either a 36V or 48V solenoid depending on the pack. Installing the wrong voltage rating will cause the solenoid to burn out quickly. The physical connectors are usually the same; voltage is marked on the solenoid body.
